Stroke Predictions¶

A stroke occurs when blood vessels in the brain bursts or become blocked. This can cause lasting brain damage, long-term disability, or even death. A close family member's recent bout of stroke has interested me in learning more about the causes of this disease and subsequently what can be done to prevent it. In this notebook, I will be using neural networks to predict whether a patient will become diagnosed with stroke given a set of features.

The Data¶

I will be using data from a Kaggle data set:

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/prosperchuks/health-dataset

Feature Columns¶

  • sex – 1: male; 0: female
  • age – patient's age (in years)
  • hypertension – patient has ever had hypertension (1) or not (0)
  • heart_disease – patient has ever had heart_disease(1) or not (0)
  • ever_married – patient married (1) or not (0)
  • work_type – patient job type: 0 - Never_worked, 1 - children, 2 - Govt_job, 3 - Self-employed, 4 - Private
  • Residence_type – patient area: 1 - Urban, 0 - Rural
  • avg_glucose_level – patient average blood sugar level
  • bmi – Body Mass Index
  • smoking_status – 1 - smokes, 0 - never smoked
  • stroke – Whether the patient has stroke (1) or not (0)

Conclusion¶

I initially started this project hoping to enlighten myself on the causes of stroke. I really expected age to play a larger part than it did in the dataset. Perhaps my initial assumptions were wrong, or maybe that the data deliberately collected stroke and nonstroke cases from a wide range of ages. Or that stroke only starts appearing frequently at a very large age and the age range here does not capture it. Nevertheless, hyptertension, heart_disease, ever_married, avg_glucose_level, and sex are significant risk factors for stroke.

Outside Research¶

Other online sources, such CDC, corroborate these findings. Hypertension, or high blood pressure, serves as a main risk factor for stroke. Women are at higher risk for strokes because they tend to live longer than men. Obesity and diabetes, both related to blood glucose levels and bmi, are other risk factors. ever_married was a feature that I was initially surprised had moderate correlation, but makes sense if we consider that stress causes high blood pressure. Thus, frequent exercise, healthier eating habits, and calming activities are good ways for reducing the risk of a stroke.

Overall, I am proud that my model did well. These results are intriguing and may be a starting point for further research.
